# Cover Letter — Fire Technology (Research article)

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

Please consider our manuscript, **"Fatal Fire Incidents in Iceland, 1968–2025: A National Registry Study of Building Construction Period and Fire Mortality,"** for publication in *Fire Technology*. We submit it under the **Research** article type; it is a compact but complete original-research study.

The study introduces the Gjöll registry, the first national incident-level record of every fatal fire in Iceland — all 113 fatal incidents (145 deaths) across 58 years, each traceable to its documentary sources. Two findings are of direct relevance to fire-safety practice and engineering. First, fire mortality declined roughly 80% from its 1970s peak (21.9 deaths per million per year) to 4–7 per million from the 1990s onward. Second, fatal fires concentrate strongly in older building stock: the fatal-fire rate in buildings of recorded construction year 1999 or later is significantly lower than in pre-1998 dwellings, with an exact Poisson rate ratio whose 95% confidence interval excludes 1 under every reasonable convention for classifying a fatal fire in a building (including a deliberately adversarial worst case). We are careful to frame this as a construction-period cohort description: it is compatible with a safety benefit of code-era construction but does not, and cannot, identify the independent causal effect of any single regulation.

The contribution is primarily the registry itself as auditable, updatable research infrastructure, together with a transparently bounded characterization of where residual fatal-fire risk now sits. Every reported statistic is reproducible from deposited data by an accompanying verification script that runs in continuous integration. The registry is deposited at DOI 10.34881/I5WGJU with a public interface at www.gjoll.is, and all analysis code is openly available at github.com/Magnussmari/gjoll-fire-technology. Reporting follows STROBE with the RECORD extension for routinely collected data (checklist attached). Extended methods, full robustness analyses, and an institutional discussion are provided as Supplementary Material.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has been approved by both authors. We have no competing interests beyond the disclosed prior professional association of the corresponding author with the Icelandic Housing and Construction Authority, detailed in the Declarations.
