Dear Editor-in-Chief,

Please consider our manuscript, "Fatal Fire Incidents in Iceland, 1968–2025: Long-Term Trends, Building Age, and the Effectiveness of Fire Safety Regulation," for publication in Fire Technology.

This study presents a national 58-year incident-level dataset on fatal fire incidents in Iceland, combining descriptive fire epidemiology, construction-year stratification, person-years rate approximation, and interrupted time series modeling. The core empirical result is that in the period with complete construction-year recording, no fatal structure-fire incidents occurred in post-1998 buildings, alongside a sustained long-term mortality decline and persistent seasonal concentration during the heating period.

The manuscript goes beyond statistical description to examine the institutional effectiveness of Iceland's fire safety regulatory framework, drawing on the governance literature to assess whether the documented gains are sustainable. A new section on "paper versus practice" addresses the gap between regulatory outputs and measurable outcomes, informed by the lead author's decade of operational experience as a firefighter and emergency medical technician.

We believe the manuscript is a strong fit for Fire Technology because it addresses fire safety in the built environment, links outcomes to building and regulatory context, and contributes to evidence-based fire safety policy evaluation.

The manuscript is original, not under consideration elsewhere, and approved by all authors. Data and reproducibility materials are publicly available (DOI: 10.34881/I5WGJU).
