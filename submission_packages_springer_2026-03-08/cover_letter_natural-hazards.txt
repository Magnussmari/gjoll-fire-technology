Dear Editors,

We submit the manuscript "Fatal Fire Mortality as a Long-Term Urban Hazard in Iceland (1968–2025): Seasonality, Building Stock Age, and Regulatory Effectiveness" for consideration in Natural Hazards.

The paper presents a national longitudinal hazard dataset and examines fire mortality through risk assessment and mitigation lenses. We quantify long-term change, heating-season concentration, and building-age vulnerability, and apply interrupted time series analysis around a major regulatory transition. The analysis extends beyond statistical description to evaluate the institutional effectiveness of Iceland's fire safety framework, including the sustainability of documented gains under conditions of organizational restructuring.

The manuscript aligns with Natural Hazards by combining empirical hazard characterization, risk-relevant stratification, and mitigation-oriented interpretation. It offers transferable lessons on how regulatory and infrastructural transitions affect rare but high-consequence mortality outcomes in cold-climate urban systems.

This manuscript is original, not under review elsewhere, and approved by all authors. Data and reproducibility materials are publicly available (DOI: 10.34881/I5WGJU).
