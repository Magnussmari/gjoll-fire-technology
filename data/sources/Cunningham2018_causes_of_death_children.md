# Major Causes of Death in Children and Adolescents in the United States

**Full citation:** Cunningham, R.M., Walton, M.A., Carter, P.M. (2018). The Major Causes of Death in Children and Adolescents in the United States. *New England Journal of Medicine*, 379(25), 2468-2475.

**DOI:** [10.1056/NEJMsr1804754](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMsr1804754)

**Type:** Journal article (review)

**Bib key:** Cunningham2018

## Abstract

Comprehensive review of causes of death among children and adolescents (ages 1-19) in the United States, analyzing CDC WONDER mortality data. Documents that injuries (both intentional and unintentional) account for more than 60% of deaths in this age group, and provides trend data showing significant changes over the 1999-2016 period.

## Key Findings

- 20,360 deaths among US children and adolescents (ages 1-19) in 2016.
- Over 60% of deaths were injury-related.
- Fire/burns ranked as the 10th leading cause of death (340 deaths, 1.7% of total in 2016).
- Deaths from residential fires fell 73% between 1990 and 2016.
- The decline in fire deaths is attributed to three concurrent factors: decreased smoking rates, increased smoke detector installation, and improved building fire safety codes.
- Motor vehicle crashes remained the leading cause of death; firearm injuries were second.

## Key Data Points

- 340 fire/burn deaths among ages 1-19 in 2016 (10th leading cause, 1.7%)
- 73% decline in residential fire deaths, 1990-2016
- Three-factor attribution: reduced smoking + more smoke detectors + better building codes
- 20,360 total deaths ages 1-19 in 2016; >60% injury-related
