# Fire Fatalities in Ireland Using Coronial Data

**Full citation:** Doyle, A., Lyons, S., Lynn, E. (2019). Profile of fire fatalities in Ireland using coronial data. *Fire Safety Journal*, 110, 102892.

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.firesaf.2019.102892](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.firesaf.2019.102892)

**Type:** Journal article

**Bib key:** Doyle2019

## Abstract

Epidemiological profile of 106 fire fatalities in Ireland over the three-year period 2014-2016, using linked coronial data including toxicology results, inquest records, and fire investigation reports. Provides detailed demographic, toxicological, temporal, and spatial characteristics of fire deaths.

## Key Findings

- 106 fire fatalities in Ireland, 2014-2016.
- 65% male, median age 63 years, 52% aged 65 or older.
- 92% of deaths occurred in private dwellings.
- 69% of decedents were alone at the time of the fire.
- Alcohol was prominent: 51% of decedents with toxicology results had positive blood alcohol levels.
- Smoking materials were a leading ignition source.
- More fatal fires occurred at night and during winter months (November peak).
- Rural areas overrepresented relative to population share.
- Only 17% of fire-death dwellings had working smoke alarms.
- Carbon monoxide poisoning was the leading cause of death.

## Key Data Points

- 106 fatalities over 3 years (Ireland, 2014-2016)
- 65% male; 52% aged 65+
- 92% in private dwellings; 69% alone at time of fire
- 51% positive blood alcohol on toxicology
- 17% had working smoke alarms
- Seasonal peak: November; more fatal fires at night
