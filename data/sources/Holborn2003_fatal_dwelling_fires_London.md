# Fatal Dwelling Fires in London (1996-2000)

**Full citation:** Holborn, P.G., Nolan, P.F., Golt, J. (2003). An analysis of fatal unintentional dwelling fires investigated by London Fire Brigade between 1996 and 2000. *Fire Safety Journal*, 38(1), 1-42.

**DOI:** [10.1016/S0379-7112(02)00049-8](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0379-7112(02)00049-8)

**Type:** Journal article

**Bib key:** Holborn2003

## Abstract

Analysis of 381 fatal unintentional dwelling fires investigated by London Fire Brigade between 1996 and 2000, resulting in 418 deaths. The study examines the circumstances, causes, and risk factors associated with fatal dwelling fires in the Greater London area, drawing on detailed fire investigation reports.

## Key Findings

- 381 fatal fires resulting in 418 deaths in London over 5 years (1996-2000).
- 86% of fatal fires occurred in residential dwellings.
- Smoking materials were the ignition source in 47% of fatal fires.
- Alcohol involvement was a major contributing factor.
- Overrepresented risk groups: elderly, disabled, those living alone.
- Social deprivation was associated with higher fire fatality risk.
- Not having a working smoke alarm substantially increased fatality risk.
- Seasonal concentration during winter months.
- Carbon monoxide poisoning was the predominant mechanism of death.

## Key Data Points

- 381 fatal fires, 418 deaths (London, 1996-2000)
- 47% ignition by smoking materials
- 86% in residential dwellings
- Elderly, disabled, alcohol-impaired, and socially deprived populations overrepresented
