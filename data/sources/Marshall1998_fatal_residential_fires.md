# Fatal Residential Fires: Who Dies and Who Survives?

**Full citation:** Marshall, S.W., Runyan, C.W., Bangdiwala, S.I., Linzer, M.A., Sacks, J.J., Butts, J.D. (1998). Fatal Residential Fires: Who Dies and Who Survives? *JAMA*, 279(20), 1633-1637.

**DOI:** [10.1001/jama.279.20.1633](https://doi.org/10.1001/jama.279.20.1633)

**Type:** Journal article

**Bib key:** Marshall1998

## Abstract

Case study of all fatal residential fires in North Carolina over a 12-month period (February 1988 - January 1989), comparing characteristics of those who died with those who survived in the same fires. Of 332 persons present in 155 fatal fires, 190 (57%) died. A "high-vulnerability" group (age <5 or >64, physical/cognitive disability, alcohol/drug impairment) had an odds ratio of 4.01 for death. Functioning smoke detectors lowered death risk (OR 0.39), and the presence of an able-bodied potential rescuer reduced risk for the high-vulnerability group (OR 0.49).

## Key Findings

- 155 fatal residential fires in North Carolina over 12 months; 332 persons present, 190 died (57%).
- 65% of decedents were male; mean age 39 years.
- 41% (78/190) of decedents were home alone at time of fire.
- 53% of adult decedents with blood alcohol measured were intoxicated (BAC >100 mg/dL).
- High-vulnerability group (age <5 or >64, disability, alcohol/drug impairment): OR 4.01 for death (95% CI: 2.29-7.03).
- Functioning smoke detector: adjusted OR 0.39 for death (95% CI: 0.18-0.83), approximately 60% risk reduction.
- Smoke detectors equally effective in both low- and high-vulnerability populations.
- Presence of a potential rescuer reduced death risk for vulnerable group: OR 0.49 (95% CI: 0.24-0.99).
- Carbon monoxide poisoning was the most common mechanism of death (65%).
- Only 25% of households had a smoke detector installed; of those, only about half were operational.

## Key Data Points

- 155 fatal fires, 190 deaths, 142 survivors (North Carolina, 1988-1989)
- Smoke detector OR = 0.39 (adjusted), ~60% risk reduction
- High-vulnerability OR = 4.01; children <5: OR 6.0 for death
- 53% of adult decedents intoxicated
- 25% of households had smoke detectors; many non-operational
